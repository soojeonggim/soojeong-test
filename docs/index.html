Queen - Bohemian Rhapsody (Live Aid 1985)

1970

Formation of Queen

Freddie Mercury, Brian May, Roger Taylor, and John Deacon come together to form Queen, marking the beginning of a new era in rock music.

1973

Release of Debut Album

Release of their self-titled debut album, "Queen."

1975

A Night at the Opera

Release of "A Night at the Opera," featuring the iconic song "Bohemian Rhapsody."

1977

We Are the Champions & We Will Rock You

"We Are the Champions" and "We Will Rock You" released, becoming anthems for sporting events worldwide.

1980

The Game

Release of "The Game," featuring hits "Another One Bites the Dust" and "Crazy Little Thing Called Love."

1985

Live Aid Performance

Historic performance at Live Aid concert, which is often voted as the greatest live show in the history of rock.

1991

Freddie Mercury's Passing

Freddie Mercury passes away, just one day after publicly acknowledging he had AIDS.

1995

Made in Heaven

Posthumous album "Made in Heaven" released, featuring Freddie Mercury's last recordings.

2001

Rock and Roll Hall of Fame

Queen inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.

2004

Queen + Paul Rodgers

Queen + Paul Rodgers tour, the first tour without Freddie Mercury.

2018

Release of "Bohemian Rhapsody" movie

A biographical film about Freddie Mercury's life, detailing the years leading up to Queen's legendary appearance at the Live Aid concert in 1985, is released.
